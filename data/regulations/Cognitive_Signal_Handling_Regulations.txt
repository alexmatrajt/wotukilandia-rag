TITLE: Cognitive Signal Handling Regulations
DOCUMENT_TYPE: regulation
VERSION: 1.0
ENACTED: Cycle 3023
ISSUING_BODY: Cognitive Integrity Council of Wotukilandia

---

SECTION 1: AUTHORITY AND PURPOSE

Article 1.1: Authority
These Regulations are issued pursuant to the Telepathic Data Protection Act of Wotukilandia, Version 2.0.

Article 1.2: Purpose
These Regulations establish operational standards for the handling, storage, and interpretation of cognitive signals within Wotukilandia.

---

SECTION 2: SIGNAL CLASSIFICATION

Article 2.1: Signal Categories
Cognitive signals shall be classified into the following categories:
(a) Surface Signals;
(b) Reflective Signals;
(c) Core Signals;
(d) Residual Echoes.

Article 2.2: Classification Requirement
All collected signals must be classified prior to processing.

Article 2.3: Misclassification
Improper classification resulting in unauthorized access shall be treated as a violation of Article 3.1 of the Telepathic Data Protection Act of Wotukilandia.

---

SECTION 3: CONSENT VERIFICATION PROTOCOLS

Article 3.1: Layer Matching
Consent must correspond to the classification level of the signal accessed.

Article 3.2: Consent Stability
Consent must be verified for temporal stability and must not degrade during processing.

Article 3.3: Fragmented Consent
Partial or fragmented consent is insufficient for accessing composite cognitive signals.

---

SECTION 4: SIGNAL STORAGE

Article 4.1: Storage Integrity
Cognitive signals must be stored in their original structure without compression that alters meaning.

Article 4.2: Echo Degradation
Residual echoes must be labeled with a degradation index indicating reliability.

Article 4.3: Storage Duration
Core signals may not be stored beyond 3 standard cycles without renewed authorization.

---

SECTION 5: ACCESS LOGGING

Article 5.1: Immutable Logs
All access events must be recorded in an immutable log system.

Article 5.2: Required Fields
Logs must include:
(a) identity of accessing entity;
(b) signal classification;
(c) consent layer;
(d) timestamp.

Article 5.3: Audit Requests
Logs must be accessible to individuals exercising their right to cognitive audit under Article 5.2 of the Telepathic Data Protection Act of Wotukilandia.

---

SECTION 6: PROHIBITED PRACTICES

Article 6.1: Signal Reconstruction
Reconstruction of incomplete cognitive signals into assumed full memories is prohibited.

Article 6.2: Cross-Signal Blending
Combining signals from multiple individuals without explicit layered consent is prohibited.

Article 6.3: Silent Analysis
Analyzing cognitive signals without recording access in logs is prohibited.

---

SECTION 7: ENFORCEMENT

Article 7.1: Monitoring
The Cognitive Integrity Council of Wotukilandia shall conduct periodic audits.

Article 7.2: Violations
Violations may trigger suspension of cognitive processing privileges.

---

SECTION 8: FINAL PROVISIONS

Article 8.1: Interpretation
These Regulations shall be interpreted consistently with the Telepathic Data Protection Act of Wotukilandia.

Article 8.2: Effective Cycle
These Regulations take effect immediately.

---
