TITLE: Cooperative Activity Compliance Guidelines
DOCUMENT_TYPE: regulation
VERSION: 1.0
ENACTED: Cycle 3023
ISSUING_BODY: Ministry of Collective Activity

---

SECTION 1: AUTHORITY AND PURPOSE

Article 1.1: Authority
These Guidelines are issued pursuant to the Interstellar Labor and Cooperative Activity Act, Version 2.0.

Article 1.2: Purpose
These Guidelines define compliance expectations for cooperative labor structures and contributor protections.

---

SECTION 2: CONTRIBUTION TRACKING

Article 2.1: Contribution Records
All cooperative activity must maintain records of contributions.

Article 2.2: Cognitive Contribution
Cognitive labor must be recorded in a manner that reflects intensity and duration.

Article 2.3: Attribution Disputes
Disputes regarding contribution must rely on recorded data unless overridden by mutual agreement.

---

SECTION 3: CONSENSUS SYSTEMS

Article 3.1: Declared Consensus Model
Each cooperative group must declare its consensus threshold.

Article 3.2: Dynamic Adjustment
Consensus thresholds may change if all active contributors agree.

Article 3.3: Silent Contributors
Entities that do not actively participate in decision-making shall not be assumed to consent.

---

SECTION 4: EXIT PROTOCOLS

Article 4.1: Exit Notice
Contributors must provide notice unless immediate exit is justified by harm or instability.

Article 4.2: Transitional Obligations
Exiting contributors must transfer essential knowledge required for continuity.

Article 4.3: Forced Retention
No entity may be prevented from exiting a cooperative structure.

---

SECTION 5: VALUE DISTRIBUTION

Article 5.1: Distribution Models
Cooperatives must define how shared output is distributed.

Article 5.2: Cognitive Weighting
Cognitive labor must not be undervalued relative to physical labor.

Article 5.3: Delayed Distribution
Delayed compensation must be justified and documented.

---

SECTION 6: DISPUTE HANDLING

Article 6.1: Harmonic Mediation Requirement
All disputes must first undergo Harmonic Mediation.

Article 6.2: Escalation Criteria
Escalation is permitted only after mediation fails or is deemed ineffective.

Article 6.3: Evidence Standards
Evidence derived from cognitive signals must comply with the Telepathic Data Protection Act and related regulations.

---

SECTION 7: OVERSIGHT

Article 7.1: Compliance Reviews
The Ministry of Collective Activity may conduct periodic compliance reviews.

Article 7.2: Adaptive Interpretation
These Guidelines may be updated to reflect evolving cooperative practices.

---

SECTION 8: FINAL PROVISIONS

Article 8.1: Non-Binding Nature
These Guidelines are binding unless explicitly designated as advisory in specific provisions.

Article 8.2: Effective Cycle
These Guidelines take effect upon issuance.

---
