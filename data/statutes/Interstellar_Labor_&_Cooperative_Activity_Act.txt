TITLE: Interstellar Labor and Cooperative Activity Act
DOCUMENT_TYPE: statute
VERSION: 1.0
ENACTED: Cycle 3020
AUTHORITY: The Harmonic Assembly of Zephyria

---

SECTION 1: PURPOSE AND STRUCTURE

Article 1.1: Purpose
This Act governs all forms of labor, including physical, cognitive, and distributed cooperative activity.

Article 1.2: Non-Hierarchical Principle
Work relationships shall not require fixed hierarchical structures and may operate through consensus networks.

Article 1.3: Constitutional Basis
This Act implements equality and due process protections under Articles 2.3 and 2.5 of the Constitution.

---

SECTION 2: DEFINITIONS

Article 2.1: Contributor
Any sentient being or distributed intelligence participating in productive activity.

Article 2.2: Coordinating Entity
An entity responsible for organizing or facilitating contributions.

Article 2.3: Shared Output
The result of collaborative labor, regardless of form or medium.

Article 2.4: Cognitive Labor
Work performed through thought, analysis, or telepathic interaction.

---

SECTION 3: RIGHTS OF CONTRIBUTORS

Article 3.1: Right to Fair Value Exchange
All contributors are entitled to compensation proportional to their contribution, including cognitive labor.

Article 3.2: Right to Exit
Contributors may withdraw from cooperative activity without penalty, subject to agreed transition conditions.

Article 3.3: Right to Identity Integrity
No contributor may be required to alter their cognitive or biological structure as a condition of participation.

---

SECTION 4: COOPERATIVE STRUCTURES

Article 4.1: Fluid Agreements
Work arrangements may evolve dynamically without fixed contracts, provided changes are consensual and recorded.

Article 4.2: Consensus Thresholds
Groups may establish thresholds for decision-making, including majority, supermajority, or full consensus.

Article 4.3: Distributed Presence
Entities operating across multiple bodies or nodes shall be treated as a single contributor unless otherwise specified.

---

SECTION 5: DISPUTES

Article 5.1: Harmonic Mediation
Disputes shall first be addressed through Harmonic Mediation, a non-adversarial resolution process.

Article 5.2: Escalation
Unresolved disputes may be brought before the Labor Tribunal.

Article 5.3: Cognitive Evidence
Cognitive signals may be used as evidence only in accordance with the Telepathic Data Protection Act.

---

SECTION 6: TERMINATION AND DISSOLUTION

Article 6.1: Dissolution of Cooperation
Cooperative arrangements may dissolve upon consensus or material breakdown of function.

Article 6.2: Residual Value Distribution
Remaining value must be distributed fairly among contributors.

Article 6.3: Memory of Contribution
Records of contribution must be preserved to ensure future claims.

---

SECTION 7: OVERSIGHT

Article 7.1: Ministry of Collective Activity
This Act is overseen by the Ministry of Collective Activity.

Article 7.2: Adaptive Guidelines
The Ministry may issue non-binding guidance reflecting evolving labor structures.

---

SECTION 8: FINAL PROVISIONS

Article 8.1: Relationship to Other Laws
This Act shall be interpreted consistently with the Constitution and applicable statutes.

Article 8.2: Effective Cycle
This Act takes effect upon enactment.

---
