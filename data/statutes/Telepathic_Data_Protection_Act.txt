TITLE: Telepathic Data Protection Act
DOCUMENT_TYPE: statute
VERSION: 1.0
ENACTED: Cycle 3022
AUTHORITY: The Harmonic Assembly of Zephyria

---

SECTION 1: PURPOSE AND SCOPE

Article 1.1: Purpose
This Act governs the lawful interaction with cognitive signals, including extraction, resonance mapping, and memory echo reconstruction.

Article 1.2: Scope
This Act applies to all entities operating within Zephyrian jurisdiction, including distributed consciousness entities and multi-body intelligences.

Article 1.3: Constitutional Basis
This Act implements the protections established under Article 2.2 of the Constitution of Zephyria.

---

SECTION 2: DEFINITIONS

Article 2.1: Cognitive Signal
Any measurable or perceivable output of a sentient mind, including thoughts, impressions, and residual memory echoes.

Article 2.2: Memory Echo
A residual cognitive imprint that persists after a thought has occurred, including involuntary projections.

Article 2.3: Resonance Mapping
The process of aligning one cognitive field with another to interpret or extract meaning.

Article 2.4: Layered Consent
Consent granted at multiple cognitive depths, including surface, reflective, and core layers.

---

SECTION 3: LAWFUL ACCESS

Article 3.1: Requirement of Layered Consent
Access to cognitive signals requires consent at the appropriate cognitive layer relevant to the depth of access.

Article 3.2: Invalid Consent
Consent is invalid if obtained during:
(a) cognitive instability;
(b) externally induced emotional override;
(c) temporal desynchronization.

Article 3.3: Passive Reception
Unintentional reception of stray cognitive signals is not unlawful, provided no recording or analysis occurs.

---

SECTION 4: RESTRICTED PRACTICES

Article 4.1: Deep Memory Extraction
Extraction of core-layer memories is prohibited unless authorized by a Tri-Mind Warrant.

Article 4.2: Collective Interrogation
Simultaneous resonance mapping of multiple individuals is prohibited except in declared interstellar emergencies.

Article 4.3: Predictive Cognition Profiling
The use of cognitive data to predict future behavior is restricted and requires explicit judicial authorization.

---

SECTION 5: RIGHTS OF SENTIENT BEINGS

Article 5.1: Right to Cognitive Silence
Individuals may shield their thoughts from all external access without justification.

Article 5.2: Right to Cognitive Audit
Individuals may request a record of all entities that have accessed their cognitive signals.

Article 5.3: Right to Memory Correction
Individuals may request correction or deletion of stored memory echoes where distortion is demonstrated.

---

SECTION 6: OBLIGATIONS OF OPERATORS

Article 6.1: Signal Integrity
Entities handling cognitive data must preserve the original structure of the signal.

Article 6.2: Distortion Liability
Any alteration of cognitive signals that results in harm creates strict liability.

Article 6.3: Transparency Logs
All cognitive interactions must be recorded in an immutable log accessible to oversight bodies.

---

SECTION 7: OVERSIGHT AND ENFORCEMENT

Article 7.1: Cognitive Integrity Council
This Act is enforced by the Cognitive Integrity Council.

Article 7.2: Tri-Mind Warrants
Authorization for intrusive access requires approval from three independent judicial minds.

Article 7.3: Penalties
Violations may result in cognitive access bans, operational dissolution, or identity suspension.

---

SECTION 8: TEMPORAL PROVISIONS

Article 8.1: Retroactive Memory Use
Recovered memory echoes from past cycles may not be used as evidence unless verified for temporal distortion.

Article 8.2: Future-State Projections
Predicted cognitive states may not be treated as factual evidence.

---

SECTION 9: FINAL PROVISIONS

Article 9.1: Relationship to Other Laws
This Act prevails in matters involving cognitive signals.

Article 9.2: Effective Cycle
This Act takes effect immediately upon enactment.

---
