CASE_TITLE: In re Xel’thar Incident Review
COURT: Supreme Court of Wotukilandia
CYCLE: 3025
DOCUMENT_TYPE: case_law

---

SECTION: FACTS

Following an interstellar incident, investigators relied on residual memory echoes collected from witnesses.

The memory echoes showed signs of degradation and temporal distortion.

The reviewing body admitted the echoes as primary evidence in determining liability.

---

SECTION: ISSUE

Whether degraded memory echoes may be used as reliable evidence in legal proceedings.

---

SECTION: HOLDING

Degraded or temporally distorted memory echoes cannot be relied upon as primary evidence without verification.

---

SECTION: REASONING

The Court cited Article 8.1 of the Telepathic Data Protection Act of Wotukilandia, which restricts the use of retroactive memory echoes unless verified for distortion.

It also referenced Article 4.2 of the Cognitive Signal Handling Regulations of Wotukilandia, requiring degradation indexing.

The Court found that the absence of reliability verification rendered the evidence insufficient.

The decision emphasized that cognitive evidence must meet higher integrity standards due to its mutable nature.

---

SECTION: PRECEDENTIAL EFFECT

This decision limits the use of memory echoes and requires verification mechanisms before admissibility in Wotukilandia.
