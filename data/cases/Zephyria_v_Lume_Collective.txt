CASE_TITLE: Zephyria v. Lume Collective
COURT: Supreme Court of Zephyria
CYCLE: 3024
DOCUMENT_TYPE: case_law

---

SECTION: FACTS

The Lume Collective, a distributed intelligence entity, conducted resonance mapping on multiple individuals to extract core-layer memories related to a security breach.

The Collective argued that partial consent had been obtained from participants during a cooperative investigation process.

Several individuals later claimed that their consent was limited to surface-level cognitive access and did not extend to deep memory extraction.

---

SECTION: ISSUE

Whether layered consent permitting surface-level access can justify extraction of core-layer memories under the Telepathic Data Protection Act.

---

SECTION: HOLDING

Layered consent must explicitly match the depth of cognitive access. Surface-level consent does not authorize core-layer memory extraction.

---

SECTION: REASONING

The Court held that Article 3.1 of the Telepathic Data Protection Act requires consent at the appropriate cognitive layer.

The Court further interpreted Article 4.1 of the Act, stating that deep memory extraction requires explicit authorization or a Tri-Mind Warrant.

The Collective’s reliance on generalized or inferred consent was insufficient.

The Court emphasized that layered consent is not hierarchical permission but distinct authorization levels.

---

SECTION: PRECEDENTIAL EFFECT

This decision establishes that consent must be explicitly tied to the depth of cognitive access and cannot be inferred across layers.
