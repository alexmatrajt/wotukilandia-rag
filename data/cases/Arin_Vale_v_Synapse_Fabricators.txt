CASE_TITLE: Arin Vale v. Synapse Fabricators
COURT: Zephyrian Labor Tribunal (Appellate Division)
CYCLE: 3023
DOCUMENT_TYPE: case_law

---

SECTION: FACTS

Arin Vale contributed cognitive design work to a cooperative project managed by Synapse Fabricators.

The cooperative used a distribution model that heavily favored physical contributions over cognitive input.

Vale argued that their cognitive labor, which defined the core architecture of the project, was undervalued.

---

SECTION: ISSUE

Whether a cooperative may assign disproportionately lower value to cognitive labor compared to physical labor.

---

SECTION: HOLDING

Cognitive labor must be valued equitably and cannot be systematically undervalued.

---

SECTION: REASONING

The Tribunal relied on Article 5.2 of the Cooperative Activity Compliance Guidelines, which prohibits undervaluation of cognitive labor.

It further referenced Article 3.1 of the Interstellar Labor and Cooperative Activity Act, requiring fair value exchange.

The Tribunal rejected the argument that physical outputs inherently justify higher valuation.

The decision emphasized that contribution impact, not form, determines value.

---

SECTION: PRECEDENTIAL EFFECT

This decision requires cooperatives to justify valuation models and ensures cognitive labor is treated on equal footing with physical labor.
